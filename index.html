About

You can learn all about me by downloading my resume.

I'm currently pursuing a Master of Engineering (Software) at the University of Melbourne, and will be graduating at the end of 2017. I previously completed a Bachelor of Science at the same institution.

I'm currently working as a workshop demonstrator within the Department of Computing and Information Systems, teaching the undergraduate subject Foundations of Computing. My duties include taking weekly classes to teach fundamental computer science concepts, conducting online help sessions and marking assessment tasks.

I'm the current vice president of Melbourne Universities premier tech club, the Computing and Information Systems Students Association. My role includes organising large events such as our annual Hackathon Codebrew and securing and liaising with industry sponsors. If you are a company looking to reach the top tech talent in Melbourne, don't hesitate to contact the club or myself.

I also volunteer with Spark, a non-profit that runs yearly camps for high school students from disadvantaged backgrounds that are interested in pursuing engineering. I was a mentor in both 2015 and 2016, and was member of the committee which planned and executed the 2016 camp.
